# MKT-206: Hepatoprotective Effect of Biofield Energy Treatment On Tert-Butyl Hydro Peroxide Induced Liver Injury in Hepatocellular Carcinoma Cell Line (HepG2)

**Full Title:** Hepatoprotective Effect of Biofield Energy Treatment On Tert-Butyl Hydro Peroxide Induced Liver Injury in Hepatocellular Carcinoma Cell Line (HepG2)
**Authors:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi, Snehasis Jana
**Journal:** Biomarkers & Applications, 2018 (received Nov 16, 2018, accepted Dec 17, 2018, published Dec 25, 2018)
**DOI:** 10.29011/2576-9588. 100029
**Read Date:** 2026-04-05

## Study Overview
**Type:** In vitro (hepatocellular carcinoma cell line)
**Subject:** HepG2 cells (Human Hepatoma/Hepatocellular Carcinoma Cell Line)
**Biofield Connection:** Demonstrates that The Trivedi Effect® applied to culture medium (DMEM) can protect liver hepatocytes against oxidative stress, reduce liver damage biomarkers, and restore key liver functions, suggesting consciousness-based therapy potential for hepatobiliary disorders.

## Methodology

### Cell Line:
- **HepG2:** Human hepatoma cell line used as alternative model to human hepatocytes
- **Advantages:** Easily available, cryopreservable in large quantities, maintains drug metabolizing capability upon cultivation

### Oxidative Stress Model:
- **Inducer:** tert-Butyl hydroperoxide (t-BHP) at 250 μM
- **Mechanism:** Metabolized by liver cytochrome-P450 to form free radicals → lipid peroxidation → cell injury
- **Purpose:** Standard chemical inducer for liver-injured models

### Biofield Treatment:
- **Treatment target:** DMEM (Dulbecco's Modified Eagle Medium) — **not the cells directly**
- **Healer:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi
- **Location:** USA (healer) → New Delhi, India (Dabur Research Foundation laboratory)
- **Duration:** ~3 minutes
- **Remote treatment:** No physical contact, no laboratory visit
- **Control:** Sham healer (no knowledge of Biofield Energy Healing)

### Positive Controls:
1. **Silymarin:** Known potent hepatoprotectant (concentrations: 1, 5, 25 µg/mL)
2. **Mevinolin:** HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor, cholesterol-lowering agent (concentrations: 5, 10, 20 µM)

### Study Parameters:
1. **Cell viability** (MTT assay) — safety/toxicity assessment
2. **Cytoprotective effect** against t-BHP
3. **Interleukin-8 (IL-8)** — proinflammatory cytokine
4. **Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT)** — liver enzyme, hepatocellular injury marker
5. **Cholesterol** — liver lipid metabolism
6. **Albumin** — liver protein synthesis marker

## Notable Methodology Details
- **Cell seeding density:** 10 × 10³ cells/well (96-well plate) for MTT assay
- **Incubation:** 24-48 hours at 37°C, 5% CO₂, 95% humidity
- **t-BHP concentration:** 250 μM (standardized oxidative stress inducer)
- **t-BHP exposure time:** 4 hours (acute oxidative damage model)
- **MTT protocol:** 5 mg/mL MTT, 3-hour incubation, DMSO dissolution of formazan crystals, OD at 540 nm
- **ELISA kits used:** IL-8, ALT, cholesterol, albumin (commercial kits, manufacturer's instructions)
- **Statistical analysis:** Student's t-test (two groups), One-Way ANOVA + Dunnett's post-hoc (multiple groups), p ≤ 0.05 significance
- **Sample size:** All experiments performed in triplicate (results as Mean ± SEM)

## Limitations / Considerations
- HepG2 is a cancer cell line, not normal hepatocytes (though widely used as hepatocyte alternative)
- Single t-BHP concentration tested (250 μM)
- Short observation period (24-48 hours) — no long-term chronic exposure data
- In vitro model — clinical translation requires in vivo validation
