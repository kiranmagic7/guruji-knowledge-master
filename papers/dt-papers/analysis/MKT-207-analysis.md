# MKT-207: In vitro Assessment of the Biofield Treated Test Item on Cardiac Function Using Rat Cardiomyocytes Cell Line (H9c2) via Multiparametric Analysis

**Full Title:** In vitro Assessment of the Biofield Treated Test Item on Cardiac Function Using Rat Cardiomyocytes Cell Line (H9c2) via Multiparametric Analysis
**Authors:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi, Snehasis Jana
**Journal:** Journal of Hypertension and Cardiology, 2019 (received Jan 3, 2019, accepted Feb 11, 2019, published Feb 14, 2019)
**DOI:** 10.14302/issn.2329-9487.jhc-19-2582

## Study Overview
**Type:** In vitro (rat cardiomyocytes cell line)
**Subject:** H9c2 cells (rat cardiomyocytes) — ATCC® CRL-1446™
**Biofield Connection:** Demonstrates that The Trivedi Effect® applied to culture medium (DMEM) can protect cardiac cells from oxidative stress, reduce cardiac injury biomarkers, suppress ROS, and promote healthy apoptosis. This establishes consciousness-based therapy potential for cardiovascular disorders.

**UNIQUE ASPECT:** This is the first study testing **one-time vs. two-times Biofield Energy Treatment** to assess dose-dependent effects.

## Methodology

### Cell Line:
- **H9c2:** Rat cardiomyocytes cell line (ATCC® CRL-1446™)
- **Advantages:** Alternative model to human cardiomyocytes, fewer animals required, high accuracy data

### Oxidative Stress Model:
- **Inducer:** tert-Butyl hydroperoxide (t-BHP) at 250-300 μM
- **Purpose:** Well-recognized oxidative stress inducer for in vitro cardiac assays
- **Mechanism:** Generates free radicals → inflammation → cell membrane damage → genetic material alteration → cardiovascular disease

### Biofield Treatment (NOVEL DESIGN):
- **Treatment groups:**
  1. **Untreated DMEM** (+ sham healer control)
  2. **BT-I:** One-time Biofield Energy Treated DMEM
  3. **BT-II:** Two-times Biofield Energy Treated DMEM
- **Healer:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi
- **Location:** USA (healer) → New Delhi, India (Dabur Research Foundation)
- **Duration:** ~3 minutes per treatment
- **Remote treatment:** No physical contact, no laboratory visit

### Positive Controls:
1. **Trimetazidine (TMZ):** Cardioprotective agent (0.1-100 µg/mL)
2. **N-acetyl cysteine (NAC):** Antioxidant (25-500 µg/mL)

### Study Parameters:
1. **Cell viability** (MTT assay)
2. **Cytoprotective effect** against t-BHP
3. **Lactate dehydrogenase (LDH)** — cardiac/skeletal muscle injury marker
4. **Creatine kinase-myocardial band (CK-MB)** — cardio-specific enzyme, AMI marker
5. **Reactive oxygen species (ROS)** — fluorescence assay (DCFDA)
6. **Apoptosis** (Annexin-V flow cytometry) — programmed cell death

## Key Findings

### 1. Cell Viability (MTT Assay, 24 hours):
| Treatment | % Cell Viability |
|-----------|------------------|
| Untreated DMEM | Baseline |
| **BT-I (One-time Biofield Treated)** | **89.03%** |
| **BT-II (Two-times Biofield Treated)** | **98.49%** |
| Trimetazidine (0.1-100 µg/mL) | >88% |
| N-acetyl cysteine (up to 500 µg/mL) | >74% |

**All test items showed ≥70% cell viability → safe and non-toxic**

### 2. Cytoprotective Effect Against t-BHP:

| Treatment | % Cell Viability Restoration |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| t-BHP alone | Baseline (0%) |
| **BT-I (One-time Biofield Treated)** | **16.01%** |

**Interesting:** BT-I (one-time) showed better cytoprotection than BT-II (two-times). This suggests a potential optimal dose effect.

### 3. Lactate Dehydrogenase (LDH) Inhibition:

**Both BT-I and BT-II showed ~50% inhibition of LDH — comparable protection**

### 4. Creatine Kinase-Myocardial Band (CK-MB) Inhibition:

**Background:** CK-MB increased by 4.96% in t-BHP group vs untreated (1.14 ± 0.16 nMol/min/mL baseline)

| Treatment | % Inhibition of CK-MB | Significance |

**BT-II (two-times) showed superior CK-MB protection (59.79%) vs. BT-I (49.48%)**

## Notable Methodology Details
- **Cell seeding density:** 5-20 × 10³ cells/well (96-well plate), 0.12-0.25 × 10⁶ cells/well (24/48-well plates)
- **Incubation:** 24-48 hours at 37°C, 5% CO₂, 95% humidity
- **t-BHP concentrations:** 250-300 μM (acute oxidative damage model)
- **t-BHP exposure time:** 2.5-4 hours
- **MTT protocol:** 5 mg/mL MTT, 3-hour incubation, DMSO dissolution, OD at 540 nm
- **ROS assay:** DCFDA fluorogenic substrate with horseradish peroxidase (HRP)
- **Apoptosis:** Annexin-V reagent, flow cytometry (Guava technologies)
- **Statistical analysis:** Student's t-test (two groups), One-Way ANOVA + Dunnett's post-hoc (multiple groups), p ≤ 0.05 significance
- **Sample size:** All experiments performed in triplicate (results as Mean ± SEM)

## Critical Biomarkers Explained:

**Lactate Dehydrogenase (LDH):**
- Abundant in heart and skeletal muscle
- Tetrameric enzyme responsible for anaerobic respiration
- Elevated levels indicate cardiac/muscle injury

**Creatine Kinase-Myocardial Band (CK-MB):**
- **Cardio-specific enzyme**
- **Gold standard for diagnosing Acute Myocardial Infarction (AMI)** along with troponin T (cTnI) and myoglobin (Myo)
- Sensitive and specific indicator for cardiac function

**Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS):**
- Play vital role in atherosclerosis development
- Normal cell signaling pathways require ROS
- **Excess ROS** → cell damage, cardiovascular disorders
- Imbalance of ROS production and antioxidant defense → oxidative stress

**Apoptosis (Programmed Cell Death):**
- Ensures damaged, aged, or excess cells are deleted in regulated manner
- **Inhibition of apoptotic pathway** is a potential treatment for CVD
- **Increased apoptosis is beneficial** for overall cardiovascular health (removes damaged cells before they cause harm)
