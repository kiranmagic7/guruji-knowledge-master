# MKT-199: Biofield Energy Treatment on Bone Cell Proliferation and Differentiation

**Full Title:** Effect of Biofield Energy Treatment on Bone Cell Proliferation and Differentiation for the Assessment of its Potential to Improve Bone Health

**Authors:** Trivedi MK, Branton A, Trivedi D, Nayak G, Jana S

**Journal:** Journal of Orthopedics & Bone Disorders, 2018, Volume 2, Issue 3

**DOI:** 10.23880/jobd-16000166

**Read Date:** 2026-04-04

## Study Overview

**Type:** In vitro cellular study

**Subject:** Human bone osteosarcoma cells - MG-63 (ATCC® CRL-1427™) directly treated with Biofield Energy

**Biofield Connection:** This study demonstrates The Trivedi Effect® applied directly to bone cells (not the medium), showing consciousness energy can affect cellular proliferation and differentiation processes for bone health.

## Methodology

**Instruments and Techniques:**
- MTT assay for cell viability (540 nm, Synergy HT microplate reader, BioTek, USA)
- ELISA-based ALP activity assay (405 nm)
- Direct Sirius red dye binding assay for collagen synthesis (540 nm)
- Statistical analysis: One-way ANOVA, p≤0.05 significant

**Treatment Protocol:**
- Biofield Energy Treatment applied directly to MG-63 cells in T-25 flask for ~3 minutes from ~25 cm distance at day 0
- Energy transmission without physical contact
- Cells incubated 48 hours post-treatment at 37°C, 5% CO2, 95% humidity

**Experimental Groups:**
- G1: Untreated baseline cells (200 µL phenol-free DMEM + 10% CD-FBS)
- G2: Positive control (untreated cells + β-Estradiol at 1, 10, 100 nM + 180 µL phenol-free DMEM + 10% CD-FBS)
- G3: Biofield Energy Treated cells in phenol-free DMEM + 10% CD-FBS

## Key Findings

1. **Cell viability increased by 63%** (p≤0.001) in Biofield Energy Treated group (G3) vs untreated cells (G1). Positive control showed 19%, 68%, and 53% increases at 1, 10, and 100 nM β-Estradiol respectively.

2. **Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) activity increased by 32%** in G3 vs G1. Positive control showed 38%, 35%, and 29% increases at 1, 10, and 100 nM respectively.

3. **Collagen synthesis increased by 27%** (208.3 ± 7.76 µg/mL in G3 vs 163.9 ± 2.31 µg/mL in G1, representing 127% of baseline). Positive control showed 19.3%, 10.5%, and 3.0% increases at 1, 10, and 100 nM respectively.

## Raw Data Points

**Table 1 - Cell Viability (MTT at 540 nm):**
- G1 Time T0: 0.527 ± 0.054
- G1 Time 72h: 0.910 ± 0.034 (100% proliferation)
- G2 (β-Estradiol):
  - 1 nM: 0.984 ± 0.041 (119% proliferation)
  - 10 nM: 1.170 ± 0.101 (168% proliferation, p≤0.001)
  - 100 nM: 1.114 ± 0.033 (153% proliferation, p≤0.001)
- G3: 1.150 ± 0.048 (163% proliferation, p≤0.001)

**Note:** 163% represents 63% increase over baseline.

**Table 2 - Alkaline Phosphatase Activity (405 nm):**
- G1 (Untreated): 0.212 ± 0.031
- G3: +32% vs G1
- G2 (β-Estradiol):
  - 1 nM: +38% vs G1
  - 10 nM: +35% vs G1
  - 100 nM: +29% vs G1

**Table 3 - Collagen Synthesis:**
- G1: 163.9 ± 2.31 µg/mL (100% baseline)
- G2 (β-Estradiol):
  - 1 nM: 195.7 ± 9.83 µg/mL (119.3%, p≤0.001)
  - 10 nM: 181.2 ± 4.75 µg/mL (110.5%, p≤0.001)
  - 100 nM: 168.9 ± 9.01 µg/mL (103.0%)
- G3: 208.3 ± 7.76 µg/mL (127%, p≤0.001)

All experiments in triplicates.
