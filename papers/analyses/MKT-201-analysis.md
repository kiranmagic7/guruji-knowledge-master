# MKT-201: In Vitro Assessment of Estrogenic Potential using Ishikawa Cells

**Full Title:** In Vitro Assessment of Estrogenic Potential of Biofield Energy Treatment using Human Endometrial Adenocarcinoma Cell Line

**Authors:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi, Snehasis Jana

**Journal:** Journal of Woman's Reproductive Health, 2018, Volume 2, Issue 2, pp. 12-

**DOI:** 10.14302/issn.2381-862X.jwrh-18-2459

**Read Date:** 2026-04-04

## Study Overview

**Type:** In vitro cellular study

**Subject:** Ishikawa cells (human endometrial adenocarcinoma cell line) grown in Biofield Energy Treated DMEM

**Biofield Connection:** Demonstrates The Trivedi Effect® applied to culture medium causing significant increase in alkaline phosphatase (ALP) activity—showing consciousness energy can affect estrogenic potential and endometrial function.

## Methodology

**Instruments and Techniques:**
- MTT assay for cell viability
- Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) enzyme activity measurement
- Ishikawa cell line (expresses estrogen receptor α (ERα) and progesterone receptor (PR))
- Statistical analysis: p≤0.001 threshold

**Treatment Protocol:**
- Test item: DMEM medium
- Two groups:
  1. Untreated DMEM (control)
  2. Biofield Energy Treated DMEM (by Mahendra Kumar Trivedi)

**Positive Control:** Naringenin at 500 and 1000 nM

## Key Findings

1. **Cell viability: 108%** in Biofield Energy Treated DMEM group (safe and non-toxic)
   - Positive control (Naringenin): 75% to 96% viability

2. **ALP activity increased by 73.21%** (p≤0.001) in Biofield Energy Treated DMEM vs untreated DMEM

3. **Positive control (Naringenin) ALP increases:**
   - 500 nM: 43.75%
   - 1000 nM: 200.89% (p≤0.001)

## Raw Data Points

**Cell Viability (MTT Assay):**
- Biofield Energy Treated DMEM: 108%
- Naringenin (positive control): 75-96%

**Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) Activity:**
- Untreated DMEM: 11.2% (baseline)
- Biofield Energy Treated DMEM: +73.21% (p≤0.001)
- Naringenin 500 nM: +43.75%
- Naringenin 1000 nM: +200.89% (p≤0.001)
