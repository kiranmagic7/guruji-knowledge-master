# MKT-194: Collagen Synthesis in Human Dermal Fibroblast Cells

**Full Title:** Estimation of Collagen Synthesis in Human Dermal Fibroblast Cells: an Impact of Consciousness Energy Healing Treatment
**Authors:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi, Alice Branton, Dahryn Trivedi, Gopal Nayak, Mayank Gangwar, Snehasis Jana
**Journal:** International Journal of Research Studies in Medical and Health Sciences (ISSN: 2456-6373), Volume 3, Issue 9, PP 18-24, 2018
**DOI:** [NOT FOUND]
**Read Date:** 2026-03-31

## Study Overview
**Type:** In vitro (cell line study)
**Subject:** HFF-1 (Human Foreskin Fibroblast) cell line, ATCC® SCRC-1041™, and DMEM treated with Biofield Energy
**Biofield Connection:** Demonstrates consciousness energy can significantly increase cellular proliferation and collagen production in human skin fibroblast cells, showing potential for skin health and anti-aging applications.

## Methodology
- Cell line: HFF-1 (ATCC® SCRC-1041™) — human foreskin fibroblast cells
- Growth medium: DMEM supplemented with 15% FBS
- Treatment: Biofield Energy by Mahendra Kumar Trivedi (~3 minutes, ~25 cm distance, no touching)
- Groups: I (untreated cells+DMEM), II (positive control: L-ascorbic acid 10/50 µM, 15% FBS), III (untreated cells + BT-DMEM), IV (BT-HFF-1 cells + untreated DMEM)
- Assays: MTT (cellular proliferation at 540 nm), Collagen estimation (Sirius Red dye binding)
- Incubation: BT cells kept 1 week in CO2 incubator (37°C, 5% CO2, 95% humidity); BT-DMEM stored at 4°C
- Statistics: One-way ANOVA with Bonferroni post-hoc, GraphPad Prism 4.01, p<0.05

## Key Findings
1. Cellular proliferation significantly increased by 85% (p≤0.001) in Biofield Energy Treated DMEM vs baseline control
2. Positive controls: L-ascorbic acid (10 µM) increased proliferation by 21%; 15% FBS by 44% — BT-DMEM far exceeded both
3. Collagen significantly increased by 44.2% (p≤0.01) in BT-DMEM (31.5 ± 3.08 µg/mL) vs control (21.8 ± 0.92 µg/mL)
4. BT-HFF-1 cells showed -15.3% altered collagen (18.5 ± 0.64 µg/mL) — treatment on media more effective than on cells
5. Positive control ascorbic acid showed 25% (10 µM) and 23% (50 µM) collagen increase — BT-DMEM exceeded this at 44.2%
