# MKT-195: Wound Healing Potential Using HaCaT Cell Line Scratch Assay

**Full Title:** Wound Healing Potential of Biofield Energy Treatment in HaCaT Cell Line Using Scratch Assay
**Authors:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi, Alice Branton, Dahryn Trivedi, Gopal Nayak, Mayank Gangwar, Snehasis Jana
**Journal:** Global Research Journal of Public Health and Epidemiology (ISSN: 2360-7920), Vol. 5(5), pp 136-142, September 2018
**DOI:** [NOT FOUND]
**Read Date:** 2026-03-31

## Study Overview
**Type:** In vitro (scratch wound assay)
**Subject:** HaCaT cell line (human keratinocytes) and DMEM treated with Biofield Energy
**Biofield Connection:** Demonstrates that Biofield Energy Treatment on cell growth medium significantly improves wound healing by enhancing keratinocyte migration and reducing scratch wound area — consciousness energy directly facilitates biological repair processes.

## Methodology
- Cell line: HaCaT (human keratinocytes) — procured from NCCS, Pune, India
- Treatment: Biofield Energy by Mahendra Kumar Trivedi (~3 minutes, ~25 cm distance, no touching)
- Groups: I (untreated cells + DMEM), II (positive control: Hu-EGF 30 ng/mL), III (untreated cells + BT-DMEM), IV (BT-HaCaT cells + untreated DMEM)
- Assay: In vitro scratch wound assay (48 hours) — mechanical scratch with sterile 200 µL micropipette tip
- Analysis: WimScratch Image analysis software for keratinocyte migration and wound closure area
- Incubation: BT cells 1 week in CO2 incubator (37°C, 5% CO2, 95% humidity); BT-DMEM at 4°C
- Plating: 0.08 × 10⁶ cells/well/mL in 12-well plates

## Key Findings
1. BT-DMEM group showed 35.4% cell migration vs baseline — demonstrating significant wound healing activity
2. Positive control (EGF, 30 ng/mL) showed 73.5% migration — BT-DMEM achieved ~48% of EGF's effect without any drug
3. BT-HaCaT cells showed no migration (-23.9%) — treatment on medium far superior to treatment on cells directly
4. Scratch area significantly reduced to 7.3% in BT-DMEM (from 11.3% baseline) — 35.4% wound area reduction
5. BT-HaCaT cells showed increased scratch area at 14% — worse than baseline, confirming media treatment superiority
