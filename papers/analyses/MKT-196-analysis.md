# MKT-196: Antioxidative Potential on HepG2 Cells Under Oxidative Stress

**Full Title:** Antioxidative Potential of Consciousness Energy Healing Treatment on HepG2 Cells and DMEM after Oxidative Stress Induced by Hydrogen Peroxide
**Authors:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi, Alice Branton, Dahryn Trivedi, Gopal Nayak, Sambhu Charan Mondal, Snehasis Jana
**Journal:** Journal of Antioxidant Activity (ISSN: 2471-2140), Vol-1 Issue 4, 2018
**DOI:** 10.14302/issn.2471-2140.jaa-18-2400
**Read Date:** 2026-03-31

## Study Overview
**Type:** In vitro (cell viability under oxidative stress)
**Subject:** HepG2 (human hepatoma cell line) and DMEM treated with Biofield Energy, challenged with hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂)
**Biofield Connection:** Demonstrates that consciousness energy can protect liver cells from oxidative damage — Biofield Energy Treated DMEM showed significant antioxidant protection against H₂O₂-induced stress, suggesting consciousness can modify biological medium to protect vital organs.

## Methodology
- Cell line: HepG2 (human hepatoma) — procured from NCCS, Pune, India
- Growth medium: DMEM supplemented with 10% FBS (routine), 1% FBS (experiment)
- Treatment: Biofield Energy by Mahendra Kumar Trivedi (~3 minutes, ~25 cm distance, no touching)
- Oxidative stress: 1 mM H₂O₂ challenge
- BT-HepG2 cells maintained 23 days under standard conditions before H₂O₂ challenge
- Groups: G1 (baseline: untreated cells+DMEM+1%FBS), G2 (negative control: +H₂O₂), G3 (positive control: +quercetin 10/50 µM), G4 (BT-DMEM+untreated cells+H₂O₂), G5 (BT-cells+untreated DMEM+H₂O₂)
- Assay: MTT cell viability (absorbance at 540 nm), 16 hours incubation post-H₂O₂
- Statistics: Student's t-test (between two groups), one-way ANOVA with Dunnett's test, p≤0.05

## Key Findings
1. H₂O₂ challenge reduced cell viability by 81.3% (p≤0.001) in negative control vs baseline — confirming severe oxidative damage
2. Quercetin (positive control) protected cells: 14.2% (10 µM) and 52.7% (50 µM) increased viability vs negative control (p≤0.001)
3. BT-DMEM group showed 13.6% (p≤0.001) increased cell viability vs negative control — comparable to quercetin 10 µM
4. BT-HepG2 cells showed 3.2% altered cell viability vs negative control — minimal protection when cells treated directly
5. Consistent pattern: BT-DMEM provides better protection than BT-cells, matching other GT studies
