### **LV Diastolic Dysfunction — THE KEY FINDING**

**Guruji's report:** "**LV diastolic dysfunction**" (Doppler study shows this)

#### **What is Diastolic Dysfunction?**

Diastolic dysfunction occurs when the left ventricle becomes less compliant and cannot relax/fill normally. There are four grades:

- **Grade I (Impaired Relaxation):** Mild; E/A ratio <0.8. **Nearly universal by age 60** — considered a **normal aging change** in many guidelines.
- **Grade II (Pseudonormal):** Moderate; elevated LA pressures, E/A ratio 0.8-2.0.
- **Grade III (Reversible Restrictive):** Severe; E/A ratio >2.0, high LA pressures.
- **Grade IV (Fixed Restrictive):** End-stage heart failure; poor prognosis.

#### **Normal Aging vs. Pathological Diastolic Dysfunction**

**Per Healio Cardiology Review (2015):**
> "Grade I (impaired relaxation): This is a **normal finding** and occurs in **nearly 100% of individuals by the age of 60**. The left atrial pressures are normal."

**Interpretation at age 43:**
- Guruji was **43 years old** at the time of this test
- Diastolic dysfunction at age 43 is **less common** than at age 60, but still within the spectrum of normal variation if Grade I (mild)
- **The report does not specify the grade** — absence of grading suggests it was likely **Grade I (mild impaired relaxation)**
- Grade I diastolic dysfunction **does not predict cardiovascular disease** when LA pressures are normal and systolic function is preserved
