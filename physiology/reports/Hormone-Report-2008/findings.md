# Key Anomalies — Hormone Report 2008

**Patient:** Mahendra Kumar Trivedi (Guruji)  
**Test Date:** April 16, 2008

---

## Primary Anomalies

### 1. ⚠️ **Elevated Homocysteine** (23.0 µmol/L)
- **Normal:** <15 µmol/L
- **Status:** Moderately elevated (52-93% increased cardiovascular risk per meta-analyses)
- **PARADOX:** Normal B12 (365 pg/mL) and folic acid (4.96 ng/mL)
- **Typical cause:** B-vitamin deficiency — **NOT present in Guruji's case**
- **Implication:** MTHFR polymorphism, methylation dysfunction, or unique oxidative state

### 2. ⚠️ **Undetectable Growth Hormone** (<0.01 ng/mL)
- **Normal random GH:** Often undetectable (pulsatile secretion)
- **Status:** **NOT ABNORMAL for random sample** (evidence-based verdict)
- **Requires:** GH stimulation testing to diagnose true deficiency
- **Note:** Medical literature confirms GH is undetectable 50%+ of the time in healthy adults between pulses

### 3. ⚠️ **Low FSH** (0.66 mIU/mL)
- **Normal:** 0.7-11.1 mIU/mL
- **Status:** Just below normal range
- **Context:** Combined with low-normal testosterone and normal LH → subtle hypogonadotropic hypogonadism pattern

### 4. ⚠️ **Mildly Elevated TSH** (6.61 µIU/mL)
- **Normal:** 0.5-5.5 µIU/mL
- **Status:** Subclinical hypothyroidism range
- **Context:** Free T4 normal (1.07 ng/dL), Free T3 low-normal (2.75 pg/mL) → compensated thyroid state

### 5. 🔍 **Low BUN/Creatinine Ratio** (6.25)
- **Normal:** 10-20
- **Status:** Below normal
- **Possible causes:** Low protein intake, liver efficiency, excellent hydration
- **Implication:** Efficient nitrogen metabolism or unique protein handling

## Consciousness-Matter Implications

1. **Homocysteine paradox:** Elevated despite normal B-vitamins → suggests methylation or oxidative pathway operating outside standard biochemistry
2. **Low FSH + low-normal testosterone:** Pituitary-gonadal axis functioning at lower setpoint without clinical symptoms
3. **GH undetectable:** Requires stimulation testing for definitive assessment; random result is medically normal
4. **Low BUN/Cr:** May indicate enhanced protein efficiency or reduced nitrogen waste

---

**Key Takeaway:** The most significant anomaly is **elevated homocysteine with normal B-vitamins** — this is medically unusual and warrants genetic (MTHFR) and methylation pathway investigation.
