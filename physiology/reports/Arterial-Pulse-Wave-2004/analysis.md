## Technical Deep Dive

### What is Arterial Pulse Wave Analysis?

The heart pumps blood into the aorta, creating a pressure wave that travels through arteries. When this wave hits resistance (e.g., stiff vessels, bifurcations), some energy reflects back toward the heart.

**Key measurements:**
1. **Augmentation Index (AI)** = How much the reflected wave adds to central blood pressure
   - High AI = stiff arteries (wave returns fast, adds to load on heart)
   - Low/zero AI = elastic arteries (wave returns late, minimal extra load)

2. **Aortic Reflection Time** = Time for pressure wave to travel from aorta to reflection site and back
   - Shorter time = stiffer arteries
   - Longer time = more compliant arteries

### Why This Matters

**Normal aging process:**
- Collagen/elastin degrade in arterial walls
- Arteries stiffen → earlier wave reflection → increased cardiac workload → heart enlargement, heart failure risk

**Guruji's results defy this trend:**
- At age 41, arteries showed elasticity of someone aged 16-21
- This level of vascular compliance is rarely maintained past age 30
