## Clinical Context (from educational materials included in report)

### What These Parameters Measure

1. **Ejection Duration & Buckberg SEVR**  
   Indicators of cardiac performance and efficiency of coronary blood flow.

2. **Augmentation & Aortic Reflection**  
   Indicators of peripheral and central arterial stiffness — one of the earliest parameters of atherosclerosis.

### Medical Literature Context

**Dr. Jay N. Cohn, MD** (University of Minnesota, Past President American Society of Hypertension):
> "By assessing the elasticity of the arterial system, clinical investigators have been able to identify a reduction in arterial elasticity in patients without evidence of traditional risk factors, suggesting the early presence of vascular disease. These abnormal blood vessel changes often appear to precede overt signs of cardiovascular disease and the occurrence of a heart attack or stroke by many years."

**Bronwyn A. Kingwell & C D Gatzka** (Baker Heart Research Unit, Melbourne):
> "The assessment of arterial stiffness is an attractive candidate... Arterial stiffness is associated with severity of coronary artery disease, severity of carotid artery disease, predicts future morbidity and mortality and can be assessed automatically and non-invasively."
