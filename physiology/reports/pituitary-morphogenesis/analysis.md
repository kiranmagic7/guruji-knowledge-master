### 4. ARTERIAL ELASTICITY — EXTRAORDINARY

**Observation (Dr. Marcia Emery, PhD, 2015):**
- **"Exceptionally healthy and elastic arteries"**
- Described as **"unusual for a person of his age and body habitus"**
- Age at examination: ~53 years

**Normal Human Baseline:**
- **Pulse Wave Velocity (PWV)** — gold standard for arterial stiffness:
  - Normal adults 50-60 years: 9.81 m/s median (Benczúr et al., 2009, n=17,000)
  - Healthy normotensive 45-54 years: 6.69-7.29 m/s (Argentinean cohort)
  - CMR-assessed PWV: 6.0 m/s [5.8-6.1] in healthy population (van Hout et al., 2021)
  - **PWV increases 6-8% per decade** — stiffening is age-dependent
- **Elasticity indices (normal range: 0.3-0.7)**
  - Values >0.7 indicate excessive dilation
  - Values <0.3 indicate stiffness/sclerosis
  - Healthy = mid-range elasticity maintained

**Verdict:** **EXTRAORDINARY ANOMALY**  
"Exceptionally elastic" in a 53-year-old male contradicts the universal aging pattern of arterial stiffening. Normal aging physiology shows progressive PWV increase. "Unusual for his age" from a cardiovascular specialist = outside 95th percentile for youthful arterial function.

**Citations:**
- Benczúr et al. (2009). Arteriograph study, n=17,000+ participants. (TensioMed reference)
- van Hout et al. (2021). "Normal and reference values for cardiovascular magnetic resonance-based pulse wave velocity." *Journal of Cardiovascular Magnetic Resonance*, 23. DOI: 10.1186/s12968-021-00739-y
- Reference Values Argentina (PMC4158305). "Pulse Wave Velocity in Healthy People" (2014).
